Problem

The fitness app market was crowded but broken. 15 user interviews revealed three recurring frustrations:

  • Essential features hidden behind paid subscriptions.
  • "Free" apps that offered crippled functionality.
  • A lack of trusted professionals and community support without financial barriers.

Key Insight

Health decisions are emotional and social, not purely transactional. Users didn't just want calorie counters — they wanted expert guidance and peer motivation without being nickel-and-dimed.

Solution

Carerely the first truly free, social-native fitness platform.

MVP Validation

Built a WordPress MVP using BuddyBoss (a white-label social networking software). This gave us community forums, user profiles, and content sharing out-of-the-box, allowing us to test demand in just weeks.

Final Platform

Once we had product market fit, we then built the full product launch which included comprehensive calorie tracking, visual progress graphs, certified nutritionist content, and community features.

Target Audience

  • Demographics: Adults ages 18-35 (younger millennial/Gen Z)
  • Income: Lower to moderate income, budget-conscious
  • Psychographics: Fitness-motivated but cost-sensitive, values authenticity and community
  • Behavior: Has tried multiple fitness apps, abandoned paid subscriptions due to cost
  • Pain Points:
    • Frustrated with limited "free" app functionality
    • Wants professional guidance without premium costs
    • Seeks genuine community support, not just solo tracking

Execution & Results

Strategic decisions and measurable outcomes that drove success

Team Leadership

Led a 6-person development team. Prioritized features using a user impact vs. technical complexity matrix.

Strategic Decisions

Created a contractor-based system where nutritionists provide content, allowing us to offer professional guidance for free to users while giving nutritionists direct access to their target audience.
